## 1.  Overview

## 1.1 Objectives

Lean Technologies cybersecurity strategy is a comprehensive plan that encompasses  various components,  including  alignment  with  regulatory  compliance,  a  focus  on  people,  processes,  and technology,  reducing  vulnerabilities,  improving  threat intelligence, and leveraging cutting edge tools and human expertise in the security domain for enhanced security.

## 1.2 Vision and Mission

Vision -  To  provide  a  trusted  and  resilient  cyberspace  that  allows  Lean  to  become  the  most  secure platform in the FinTech world.

Mission - To secure infrastructure, protect data and financial data, be good stewards of our clients PII and educate our employees and clients about good cyber hygiene.

## 1.3 Cybersecurity Strategy Objectives and Goals

- o Ensuring data protection and privacy
- o Ensuring  application/SW  security,  infrastructure  security,  IT  and  enterprise  security  for  Lean platforms, products and services
- o Protecting Lean from cyber security incidents and breaches
- o Client and employees cyber education
- o Security Due Diligence for 3 rd  party vendor management
- o Ensure high standards for physical security.
- o Maintain compliance against applicable cybersecurity requirements, laws, regulations, including but  not  limited  to  SAMA  CSF,  CRFR,  MVCC,  KSA  PDPL,  UAE  IAS  and  any  additional  KSA  NCA applicable security standards.
- o Maintain company ISO27001 certification
- o Develop a proactive culture of cybersecurity awareness among employees.
- o Continuously monitor and patch any vulnerabilities in infrastructure and APIs.
- o Improve threat intelligence by monitoring global and industry-specific threats.
- o Incorporate cutting edge tools for threat detection and response.
- o Transition to a behavior-based security model, implementing Zero Trust principles.
- o Strengthen cloud security and containerization practices.

## 1.4 Alignment with Organizational and banking sector objectives

- Support Business Continuity: The cybersecurity strategy must align with the financial institution's overall  goals  of  maintaining  business  continuity,  ensuring  service  availability, and safeguarding sensitive customer information.
- Compliance with Regulatory Requirements:

- o SAMA  Compliance:  Align  with  the  Saudi  Arabian  Monetary  Authority's  cybersecurity requirements,  ensuring  continuous  monitoring,  incident  response  readiness,  and  risk mitigation strategies.
- o KSA PDPL: Ensure compliance with the KSA Personal Data Protection Law, safeguarding customer data through encryption, proper data handling, and regular audits.

## 1.5 Importance and Benefits of Cybersecurity for the Financial Institution

- Security  of  Financial  Assets:  Cybersecurity  ensures  the  protection  of  financial  transactions, customer  data,  and  the  institution's  sensitive  assets,  reducing  risks  of  financial  losses  and protecting the organization's reputation.
- Regulatory  Compliance:  Compliance  with  KSA's  regulations,  including  SAMA  (Saudi  Arabian Monetary  Authority)  requirements  :  CSF,  CRFR,  MVC,  the  KSA  PDPL  (Personal  Data  Protection Law),  and  NCA  (National  Cybersecurity  Authority)  standards,  enhances  the  institution's  legal standing and avoids penalties.
- Client  Trust  and  Confidence:  A  robust  cybersecurity  posture  builds  trust  among  customers, ensuring that their financial data and transactions are protected.
- Operational Continuity: Strong cybersecurity measures prevent business disruption from cyber-attacks, ensuring seamless banking services.
- Competitive Advantage: Demonstrating compliance with the highest cybersecurity standards can be a differentiator in the financial market, attracting security-conscious clients.

## 1.6 Anticipated Future State of Cybersecurity

- Resilient  Infrastructure:  The  organization  will  evolve  to  a  future  state  where  cybersecurity  is ingrained in all processes, ensuring rapid detection and response to emerging threats. The future will focus on building resilience against advanced persistent threats (APTs), zero-day vulnerabilities, and growing ransomware attacks.
- Zero Trust Architecture: Transitioning to a Zero Trust model, where access to systems and data is based on verification and continuous monitoring, ensuring that no entity is trusted by default.
- AI  and  Machine  Learning:  The  institution  will  leverage AI-driven  threat  detection to  identify and mitigate threats in real time, utilizing behavior-based analytics and advanced data monitoring.
- Cloud  and  Container  Security:  Strengthen  the  cloud-first  architecture  with  advanced  security measures,  encryption,  and  monitoring  to  protect  the  financial  institution's  expanding  cloud infrastructure.
- Automated Threat Response: Implement automation for incident response processes to ensure quick mitigation of detected threats, reducing manual intervention and improving response time.

- Cybersecurity  Awareness: Develop a future where all employees, from executives to junior staff, are aware of and practice good cybersecurity hygiene, minimizing risks posed by human error.

## 2.  Cybersecurity Strategy Key Components

## 2.1 Risk Management

Our  cybersecurity  strategy  places  high  importance  on  the  systematic  identification,  assessment,  and management of risks to ensure the resilience of our information assets. Our risk management framework is designed to be proactive, enabling us to anticipate and respond to evolving cyber threats effectively. We conduct regular and comprehensive risk assessments, considering both internal and external factors that may impact our security posture.

7

<!-- image -->

These assessments inform the development of risk mitigation strategies tailored to the unique needs of our  organization.  Our  approach  involves  continuous  monitoring  of  key  risk  indicators,  allowing  us  to swiftly  identify  and  address  emerging  threats.  We  prioritize  risks  based  on  their  potential  impact  on business operations and the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of sensitive information.

Clear communication channels and reporting mechanisms ensure that stakeholders are informed of risk profiles  and  mitigation  efforts.  By  embedding  risk  management  into  our  organizational  culture, we not only  strengthen  our  cybersecurity  defenses  but  also  foster  a  proactive  and  adaptive  approach  to safeguarding our digital assets.

See Lean Risk Management Framework for more details.

## 2.2 Governance and Compliance

At the core of our cybersecurity strategy is a robust governance framework that ensures the alignment of security  initiatives  with  organizational  objectives.  Our  governance  structure  is  designed  to  facilitate effective  decision-making,  risk  management,  and  accountability  across  all  levels  of  the  organization. Central to our approach is a commitment to regulatory compliance, encompassing adherence to industry standards, data protection regulations, and legal requirements.

Our governance model incorporates regular risk assessments to identify and prioritize potential threats, enabling  the  proactive  development  of  mitigation  strategies.  We  have  established  clear  roles  and responsibilities  for  cybersecurity  stakeholders,  fostering  a  culture  of  accountability  and  transparency. Through continuous monitoring and periodic evaluations, we ensure that our cybersecurity posture not only meets but exceeds regulatory expectations.

This integrated governance and compliance strategy reinforces our dedication  to  safeguarding information  assets,  maintaining  the  trust  of  stakeholders,  and  fortifying  our resilience against evolving cyber threats.

## 2.3 Incident Response

We  prioritize  a  proactive  and  well-defined  approach  to  detect,  contain,  eradicate,  and  recover  from security  events.  Clear  roles  and  responsibilities  are  established,  ensuring  a  rapid  and  coordinated response. Regular drills and scenario-based exercises enhance our team's readiness.

Continuous  improvement  is at the forefront,  with  post-incident  analyses  informing  adjustments  to  our response procedures. Through this approach, we aim to minimize the impact of incidents, protect our systems and data, and swiftly return to normal operations.

## 2.4 Security Awareness Training

Our cybersecurity  strategy  emphasizes the importance of cultivating a security-aware culture within our organization. Regular and targeted security awareness training programs empower our personnel with the knowledge and skills needed to recognize and mitigate security threats.

We provide engaging and up-to-date content, covering best practices, social engineering awareness, and compliance requirements. The goal is to create a vigilant workforce capable of safeguarding sensitive information, reducing the risk of human error, and contributing to a resilient cybersecurity posture.

## 2.6 Continuous Monitoring and Detection

Leveraging  advanced  technologies  and  proactive  methodologies,  we  continuously  scrutinize  network activities, system logs, and user behaviors. Automated alerting systems coupled with vigilant analysis by our security team allow for rapid identification of potential threats.

Regular refinement of detection rules and continuous monitoring of key risk indicators ensure our ability to  swiftly  respond  to  emerging  cybersecurity incidents. Through this vigilant approach, we enhance our situational awareness, fortify our defenses, and maintain a proactive stance against evolving threats.

We have 24x7 SOC services which provide continuous monitoring and detection across our platform and services.

## 2.7 Legal and Regulatory Landscape

Our cybersecurity  strategy  is  underpinned by a commitment to compliance with industry standards and regulatory  frameworks,  notably  ISO  27001  and SAMA cybersecurity requirements : CSF, NCA applicable standards,  CRFR,  MVC,  KSA  PDPL.  We  employ  a  comprehensive  monitoring  system  that  regularly assesses  our  practices  against  these  standards.  Continuous  adherence  to  ISO  27001  ensures  the robustness  of  our  Information  Security  Management  System  (ISMS),  while  compliance  with  SAMA requirements further safeguards our financial operations.

Regular  audits,  assessments,  and  internal  reviews  are  conducted to validate our compliance status. By prioritizing a proactive approach to regulatory adherence, we not only meet the stringent requirements of ISO 27001 and SAMA but also instill confidence in our stakeholders regarding the security and integrity of our systems and data.

We  have  established  Key  Performance  Indicators  (KPIs)  and  Key  Risk  Indicators  (KRIs)  metrics  to measure the effectiveness of the cybersecurity program as well as to be proactive with measuring certain risks level.

We  continuously  assess  the  performance  of  our  cybersecurity  program  and  strategy  and  adjust  as needed to adapt to evolving threats and challenges.

## 2.9 Third-Party Risk Management

Our cybersecurity strategy underscores the importance of a robust third-party risk management framework to safeguard our organization against external threats. We conduct thorough risk assessments, evaluating the security practices of our vendors and partners.

Our risk management approach extends to continuous monitoring, ensuring ongoing compliance with our security standards. Clear contractual requirements and regular communication channels facilitate the collaborative management of third-party risks.

Through these measures, we aim to fortify our cybersecurity defenses, protect sensitive information, and sustain a secure and resilient business ecosystem.

## 2.10 Continuous Education and Training

Our cybersecurity strategy prioritizes the continuous education and training of our personnel to cultivate a vigilant and security-conscious workforce. Regular and targeted training programs cover emerging threats, best practices, and compliance requirements.

Through ongoing education initiatives, we empower our employees to recognize and respond effectively to evolving cybersecurity challenges. By fostering a culture of continuous learning, we enhance our overall resilience against cyber threats, reduce the risk of human error, and reinforce our commitment to maintaining a secure digital environment.

## 2.11 Business Continuity Planning (BCP) and Disaster Recovery (DR)

As an integral component of our cybersecurity strategy, Lean Technologies places a strong emphasis on Business  Continuity  Planning  (BCP)  and  Disaster  Recovery  (DR)  to  ensure  the  ongoing  operational integrity and data resilience of our organization. Our BCP is aligned to the requirements of ISO22301.

Our main focus of BCP is around ensuring that the availability of our core services is maintained and as such this is why we operate a cloud first approach to enhance our redundancy measures and increase our resilience.

## 2.12 Data Security and privacy

At the center of our cybersecurity strategy is the commitment to the protection of sensitive data and the preservation of individual privacy. We  employ a comprehensive data security framework that encompasses robust encryption measures, secure data handling procedures, and access controls.

Our strategy aligns with global data protection regulations, including GDPR, KSA PDPL and other relevant mandates,  ensuring  that  personal  and  sensitive  information  is  handled  with  the  utmost  care  and compliance.  Regular  data  classification  exercises  guide  our  efforts  in  prioritizing  data  protection measures based on the sensitivity and criticality of information.

Continuous monitoring and auditing mechanisms further strengthen our data security posture, allowing us  to  promptly  identify  and  respond  to  any  potential  breaches.  Privacy by design principles govern our development  and  deployment  of  systems,  embedding  privacy  considerations  at  every  stage  of  the information  lifecycle.  Through  these  measures,  we  not  only  protect  the  confidentiality  and  integrity  of data  but  also  uphold  the  trust of our customers, partners, and stakeholders in our commitment to data security and privacy.

## 2.13 Budget and Resources

Lean  Security  team  has  a  dedicated  security  function  with  full  time  security  professional  with  strong expertise  in  security  testing,  penetration  testing,  red  teaming,  and  bug  bounty  hunting  ,  including identification  of  vulnerabilities  using  modern  techniques  and  adherence  to  the  OWASP  Top  10,  MITRE attack framework .

Our  cybersecurity strategy recognizes  the  critical  importance  of  allocating  adequate  budget  and resources  to  sustain  a  robust  defense  against  evolving  cyber  threats.  We  prioritize  investments  in state-of-the-art security technologies, regular training programs, and ongoing monitoring capabilities. This strategic allocation ensures the availability of the necessary tools and expertise to safeguard our digital assets. We remain committed to the continual evaluation and adjustment of our budgetary and resource allocations,  adapting  to  emerging  threats  and  technological  advancements  to  maintain  a  resilient cybersecurity posture.

Security  staff  performance  evaluations will be conducted on a bi-yearly basis, with a mid-year review to assess progress and provide developmental feedback, followed by a comprehensive end-of-year review to evaluate overall performance, achievements, and goal alignment.

## 2.14 Network and Architecture Security

Our  cybersecurity  strategy  prioritizes  the  fortification  of  our  network  infrastructure  to  ensure  the confidentiality,  integrity,  and  availability  of  our  critical  assets.  We  implement  a  multi-layered  approach incorporating firewalls, intrusion detection/prevention systems, and secure configurations.

Regular  network  architecture  reviews  and  vulnerability  assessments  bolster  our  defenses.  Continuous monitoring of network traffic, coupled with swift response mechanisms, enables us to detect and thwart potential threats.  Through  these  measures,  we  aim  to  maintain  a  resilient  and  secure  network environment that aligns with industry best practices and safeguards against evolving cyber threats.

We perform regular network scanning using trusted Vulnerability Assessment tools

## 2.15 Endpoint Security

We place a strong emphasis on safeguarding endpoints, recognizing them as critical gateways to our digital  environment.  Endpoint  protection  measures,  including  robust antivirus solutions, encryption, and secure configuration standards, are implemented to mitigate risks.

Proactive patch management contributes to the resilience of our systems. Through these efforts, we aim to  establish  a  secure  endpoint  landscape  that  effectively  prevents,  detects,  and  responds  to  potential security threats, ensuring the integrity of our digital infrastructure.

We  have  XDR  capabilities  through  the  deployment  of  SIEM  agents  on  our  organization  endpoints. Furthermore, we use anti-malware and anti-virus capabilities from MacOS as well as VirusTotal services.

## 2.16 Secure Software Development

Our cybersecurity  strategy integrates secure software development practices to ensure the resilience of our  digital  applications.  Following  industry  best  practices  and  leveraging  secure  coding  standards, our development teams prioritize the identification  and  mitigation  of  security vulnerabilities throughout the software  development  lifecycle.  Rigorous  testing,  code  reviews,  and  adherence  to  secure  coding guidelines contribute to the creation of robust and secure software.

<!-- image -->

We emphasize a proactive approach to cybersecurity in every phase of development, fostering a culture where  security  is  not  only  a  feature  but  an  inherent  aspect  of  our  software  development  processes. Through these measures, we aim to deliver reliable and secure software solutions that meet the highest standards of cybersecurity.

## 2.17 Assets Security

Comprehensive  asset  inventory  and  classification  processes  are  employed  to  identify  and  prioritize critical  assets.  Access  controls, encryption, and physical security measures are implemented to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of these assets.

Regular assessments and audits ensure that our security controls align with evolving threats and industry best  practices.  Through  these  measures,  we  aim to fortify our defenses, mitigate risks associated with asset compromise,  and  maintain  the  resilience  of  our  organization  in  the  face  of  cybersecurity challenges.

## 2.18 Identity and Access Management

We  enforce  robust  Identity  and  Access  Management  (IAM)  practices  to  ensure  secure  and  controlled access to our systems and data. Through centralized user authentication, authorization protocols, and least privilege principles, we aim to prevent unauthorized access and protect sensitive information.

Regular  reviews  of  user  access  rights  and  proactive management of identities contribute to a dynamic and secure IAM framework. Multi-factor authentication and strong password policies further enhance our defense  against  unauthorized  access.  By  adopting  a  comprehensive  IAM  strategy,  we  fortify  our cybersecurity  posture,  uphold  the  principle  of  least  privilege,  and  safeguard  our  digital  assets  from unauthorized use.

## 3.3.1  Internal Communication Protocols

Effective communication within the organization is critical for maintaining a strong cybersecurity posture, ensuring alignment across leadership, and facilitating compliance with regulatory requirements. The communication framework is structured to enable transparent, secure, and timely information flow across governance bodies, operational teams, and relevant stakeholders.

## 3.3.1.1 Governance and Strategic Oversight

The Risk and Compliance Committee (RCC) serves as the primary decision-making body for cybersecurity and risk management. Communication within the RCC follows a structured approach:

- Periodic Meetings and Reporting: The RCC convenes regularly to discuss cybersecurity strategy, policy approvals, risk assessments, and budget allocations. Key updates from the VP of Information Security and other security leaders are presented to ensure visibility into risk posture and incident response readiness.
- Incident and Risk Escalation: Major cybersecurity risks, regulatory non-compliance issues, or critical security incidents must be escalated promptly to the RCC by the Security Committee or the VP of Information Security, ensuring swift decision-making and mitigation.
- Regulatory and Compliance Alignment: The KSA Senior Security Manager is responsible for ensuring that compliance with SAMA, NCA, and other regulatory requirements is effectively communicated and integrated into security strategies.

## 3.3.1.2  Operational and Tactical Security Communication

The Security Committee, led by the VP of Information Security, is responsible for the execution of cybersecurity initiatives. Communication within this committee ensures real-time operational awareness and response coordination:

- Security Operations Reporting: SOC Analysts and Security Engineers provide regular updates on incident trends, emerging threats, and vulnerabilities, which are consolidated by the SOC Manager and escalated to the VP of Information Security when necessary.
- Cross-functional Collaboration: Engineering, IT, and Product teams receive security updates related to application and infrastructure security, integrating security best practices into technology development.
- Security Control Implementation: Updates on new security technologies, improvements in security posture, and compliance efforts are shared between security teams and the CTO, ensuring security remains embedded in the technology landscape.

## 3.3.1.3  Reporting Structure and Communication Flow

- VP of Information Security reports to CEO, providing strategic cybersecurity updates, incident reports, and risk assessment, engineering security.
- KSA Senior Security Manager reports directly to the CEO, focusing on SAMA compliance, bank integrations, and regulatory security requirements.
- Security Engineers and Analysts report through structured chains (SOC Manager → VP  of Infosec), ensuring technical and operational visibility across teams.
- RCC members, including Legal, Compliance, and Business leaders, receive periodic security updates, particularly when cybersecurity risks impact regulatory obligations or business continuity.

## 3.3.1.4 Employee Communication and Security Awareness

Effective internal communication extends beyond leadership and security teams to ensure that all employees are informed, engaged, and actively contributing to the organization's cybersecurity resilience. Employees play a critical role in maintaining security best practices and must be continuously educated on emerging threats and compliance requirements.

- Security Awareness Training: Regular training sessions, phishing simulations, and security workshops are conducted to enhance employee awareness of cybersecurity threats, regulatory obligations, and best practices for protecting company and customer data.
- Company-wide Security Updates: Periodic security bulletins and newsletters are distributed to keep employees informed about security policies, procedural updates, and key incidents or vulnerabilities relevant to their roles.
- Incident Reporting Mechanisms: Employees are encouraged to report security incidents, suspicious activities, or policy violations through the designated incident reporting channels (e.g., internal ticketing systems, security hotlines). A no-retaliation policy is enforced to promote proactive reporting.
- Access to Security Resources: Employees have access to security guidelines, policies, and FAQs via the company's intranet or knowledge base, ensuring that relevant information is readily available when needed.
- Cross-Departmental Collaboration: Security teams regularly engage with HR, Finance, and Operations to ensure security awareness is integrated into onboarding, policy acceptance, and compliance monitoring.

## 3.3.2  External Communication Protocols

<!-- image -->

The organization maintains structured communication with external stakeholders to ensure compliance, incident transparency, and operational coordination. Communication with these stakeholders follows predefined escalation paths, confidentiality controls, and regulatory reporting requirements.

## 3.3.2.1  Regulatory and Governmental Bodies

Communication with regulators is managed by designated security and compliance leaders, ensuring strict adherence to regulatory requirements.

- Central Bank of the UAE (CBUAE): Compliance-related security reporting, particularly regarding banking regulations and cybersecurity audits.
- Saudi Arabian Monetary Authority (SAMA): Regular reporting on cybersecurity risk assessments, incident disclosures, and adherence to SAMA-mandated security controls.
- National Cybersecurity Authority (NCA): Coordination on cybersecurity policies, reporting of critical incidents, and regulatory-driven security initiatives.
- SDAIA (Saudi Data and Artificial Intelligence Authority): Compliance with data governance, AI security policies, and national data regulations, ensuring privacy and security of financial data.

## 3.3.2.2 Clients and Business Partners

- Clients (Financial Institutions and Fintechs): Security teams provide clients with security assurance documentation, penetration test reports, and compliance attestations to establish trust in banking and fintech integrations.
- Incident Notification: In the event of a security breach impacting client data or services, a structured incident response communication plan ensures clients receive timely and accurate information, in compliance with regulatory notification timelines.

## 3.3.2.3  Crisis and Incident Communication

In the event of a major cybersecurity incident, the organization follows a Crisis Communication Plan, ensuring coordinated communication across internal and external stakeholders:

- Internal Escalation: Security teams immediately escalate incidents to the Security Committee and RCC, enabling executive-level decision-making.
- Regulatory Notifications: Compliance teams coordinate with regulators (SAMA, CBUAE, NCA) to meet reporting obligations.
- Client and Public Communication: If required, controlled messaging is released to affected clients, with legal and compliance teams ensuring adherence to disclosure policies while minimizing reputational risk.

By adhering to these structured communication protocols, the organization ensures a resilient, compliant, and well-coordinated approach to cybersecurity, risk management, and regulatory engagement.
