## 2. Introduction

Tamara Finance Company hereby going forth 'Tamara' Cyber Security Governance Committee, policy,  rules  and  procedures  prescribed  thereunder  have  been  developed  to  establish  an effective Cyber Security Governance Committee framework  that reflects and  ensures compliance with regulatory requirements.

## 2.1. Purpose

This Cyber Security Governance Committee provides an overview of Tamara's Cyber Security Governance Committees policies and practices. It outlines Tamara's Cyber Security Governance  Committee  framework  to  establish  a  common  standard  of  Cyber  Security Governance across Tamara and help manage risk related to Cyber Security Governance in line with the requirements set by the Competent Authority.

## 2.2. Scope

The  rules,  instructions  and  definition  addressed  in  this  Policy  outlines  the  main  guidelines, standards and principles governing Tamara roles and responsibilities with respect to the Cyber Security Governance Committee. The provisions of this Policy shall apply without prejudice to the  articles  of  association  of  Tamara  and  related  laws  governing  Tamara's  business  and different  activities  to  the  extent  it  is  relevant  and  appropriate. This Policy may also carry out any other duties delegated by the Board or any other Board Committees, if warranted.

## 2.3. Applicability

This Policy shall be approved by Tamara's Board of Directors and be applicable from the date of approval from Tamara's Board of Directors. This policy is applicable to Tamara in the respective jurisdictions that it operates in.

## 2.4. Ownership and Custody

Ownership: This Policy, including the requirements and guidelines contained within, shall be the sole and exclusive property of Tamara. It shall be under the ownership of the Director of Cyber Security.

Confidentiality: This information in this document shall be confidential and proprietary. It shall not be disclosed, copied, or made available to anyone other than Tamara Board of Directors, Board Committees, Executive Management and Employees of Tamara and its group entities.

Non-Disclosure:  No  part  of  this  document  shall  be  reproduced  or  transmitted  to  external parties in any form, whether electronic or manual, including photocopying recording, or in any information storage or retrieval system, without the prior written permission of the Board of Directors.

## 2.5. Review and Updates

The  Policy  shall  be  reviewed  and  approved, when needed or, at least every 2 years, by the Director  of  Cyber  Security  of  Tamara  and  shall  be  endorsed  by  Tamara's  Board of Directors. Revisions of the Policy shall be the principal way of implementing and communicating changes to  the  policies  and  procedures  that  may  arise  in  response  to  the  changing  needs  and requirements of Tamara.

Tamara's Board of Directors shall be the primary approver of revisions for the Policy to reflect the rules. A review of the Policy may also arise because of the introduction or discontinuation of  products,  services  and  operations  at  Tamara  or  to  correct  any  inadvertent  errors  or omissions that may have occurred in the preparation of this Policy.

If  the  revision  proposal  is  not  approved,  a  notification  to  that  effect  and  the  reason  will  be communicated  to  the  Director  of  Cyber  Security  by  the  Board of Directors. The log of such declines, along with reasons for decline, will be maintained by the Governance Department.

## 2.6. Implementation

This  Policy  is  effective  immediately  upon  the  Board  of  Directors  approval,  published  and communicated to all relevant stakeholders.

## 3. Committee Purpose

The  primary  purpose  of  the  Committee  is  to  assist  the  Board  of  Directors  in  fulfilling  its responsibility of oversight of:

1. Management's  responsibilities  to  regularly  assess  Tamara's  key  risks  and  engage  in enterprise-wide risk management as they relate to cyber security.
2. Tamara's  technology  and  information  systems,  including  with  respect  to  strategies, objectives,  capabilities,  initiatives,  policies,  and  investments.  The  Committee  works closely and coordinates regularly with the Risk and Credit Committee of the Board with respect  to  the  oversight  of  Tamara's  risks,  which  are  covered  by  the  scope  of  the Committee's responsibilities.

## 5.3. Committee Meetings and Documentation

The Committee shall meet regularly at least four (4) times per year or more frequently as the Committee or its Chairperson deems necessary or desirable. The Committee shall meet at such times and places as the Committee, or its Chairperson shall determine.

All committee members will be notified of the date, time, and venue of each meeting as far in advance  as  possible.  Meeting  agendas  will  be  prepared  and  circulated  to  the  members  in advance. From time to time, the Committee shall designate a Senior Member of management who  shall  act  as  management  liaison  to  the  Committee  and  work  with  the  Committee chairperson to prepare an agenda for regularly scheduled meetings.

All  significant  decisions  and  discussions  in  the  Cyber  Security  Committee shall be carefully documented. Minutes of meetings shall be prepared and circulated to all committee members as per information classification rules.

All information and minutes of meetings shared in the Committee must be classified and coded according to the following:

- Information  classified  as  "Confidential"  shall  not  be  discussed  with  any  individual outside the Committee.
- Information classified as "Restricted" shall be discussed with their direct reports based on 'need-to-know' to deal with a particular topic or risk.
- Information classified as "Internal" can be shared with all Tamara employees.

The committee's quorum shall be a minimum of five (5) members, one of whom shall be the Chairman  or his  representative.  The  Chairman  may  delegate  his  representative  to  the committee via email.

New  members  of  the  Committee  will  undergo  an  induction process to ensure that the new members  are  adequately  acquainted  with  the  Committee's  purpose,  responsibilities,  and practices. New members will be provided with a copy of the Committee's charter, information on  the  time  commitment  required,  and  specific  information  about  the  key  issues  of  the Committee.

The  Committee  is  governed  by  the  same  rules  regarding  meetings  (including  meetings  in person or by telephone or other similar communications equipment), action without meetings, notice, waiver of notice, and quorum and voting requirements as are applicable to the Board of Directors.

The Committee shall review and assess its performance and effectiveness at least annually and report its results to the Board of Directors.

## 5.4. Charter Review

The Cyber Security Governance Committee will review, at a minimum, the Committee's Charter annually  and  recommend  any  proposed  changes  to  the  Board  of  Directors  approval.  The Committee will not violate the applicable laws, regulations, or the Company's bylaws.

If  the  Committee's Charter conflicts with the laws, regulations, or the Company's bylaws, the Committee will recommend a change to bring the Committee Charter into compliance.

The  Committee  shall  prepare  and  report to the Board of Directors. the results of an annual performance  evaluation  of  the  Committee,  which  shall  compare  the  performance  of  the Committee with the requirements of this Charter.

## 6. Roles and Responsibilities

The Committee shall be responsible for the following:

- Review  the  company's  cyber  security  threat  landscape,  risks,  and  data  security programs with management, as well as its management and mitigation of cyber security risks and potential breach incidents.
- Review the Company's compliance with applicable information/ Cyber security and data protection laws with management.
- Review with management  the Company's technology and information  systems strategies and trends that may affect these strategies.
- Review  reports  and  key metrics from management on the Company's cyber security, technology and information systems and related risk management programs.
- Review  the  progress  of  major  technology-related  proposals,  plans,  projects,  and architecture decisions to ensure that these  projects  and  decisions  support  the Company's  overall business strategy and  receive appropriate support from the Company.
- Review  the capacity, performance,  and  reliability of  the  Company's  technology platforms.
- Discuss with management the Company's cyber security, technology, and information systems  policies  regarding  risk  assessment  and  risk  management,  including  the guidelines  and  policies  established  by  Tamara  to  assess,  monitor,  and  mitigate  the Company's significant cyber security, technology, and information systems-related risk exposures.
- Review  and  provide  oversight  on  the  Company's crisis preparedness with respect to cyber  security,  technology,  and  information  systems,  including  security  breach  and incident response preparedness, communication plans, and disaster recovery capabilities.
- Refer  to  the  Audit  Risk  Committee  of  the  Board  any  matters  that have come to the attention of the Committee fall under the oversight of the Audit and Risk Committee or are  otherwise  relevant  for  noting  or  consideration by the Audit and Risk Committee, including any matters relating to the Company's  internal  control  over  financial reporting.
- Periodically  review  and,  as  appropriate,  make  recommendations  to  the  Board  of Directors  regarding  the  Company's  budget,  investments,  training,  and  staffing  levels related to cyber security, technology and information systems.
- Review annually the appropriateness and adequacy of the Company's cyber-insurance coverage.

## 7. Compliance and Non - Compliance

Compliance  with  this  document  will  be  measured  and  verified  through  various  methods, including but not limited to periodic audits, assessments, and monitoring activities that adhere to  relevant Personal Data Protection Laws, SAMA, NCA, and ISO/IEC 27001 standards. These methods  may  include  business  and  cybersecurity  intelligence reports, internal and external audits,  and feedback from document owners. All compliance activities will be conducted with due regard to privacy and data protection requirements.

Any exceptions to this document must be formally documented and pre-approved by the Head of  Cyber  Security  and  Data  Protection  Officer  and  at  least  one other C-level executive within Tamara.  Any  employee found to have violated this document may be subject to disciplinary action,  up  to  and  including  termination  of  employment, in line with the Tamara's disciplinary procedures and relevant legal and regulatory frameworks.
